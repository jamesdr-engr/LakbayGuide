About the National Museum

The National Museum of the Philippines is the official repository of the country's artistic, cultural, and historical artifacts. Established in 1901, it houses collections ranging from fine arts to anthropology.

Notable exhibits include the Spoliarium by Juan Luna, ancient artifacts, and pre-colonial gold treasures.
